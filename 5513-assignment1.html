Impact of Marketing Investment on Customer Spending

Author

Qingqing Yu

Introduction

This report mainly focuses on the marketing strategies implemented by our company in the last quarter, such as advertising, email marketing, and coupons, and the marketing investment made. Based on the purchase records of customers, it examines whether higher marketing investment per customer has led to higher purchase amounts and whether there is a relationship between the two. The data mainly comes from the customer-level data of the last quarter.

Research Question

This study mainly examines whether marketing investment can lead to higher customer purchasing power, and whether there is a certain relationship between the two. In essence, it is observing the relationship between total sales investment and customer purchasing power.

Dataset Description

This dataset contains customer-level information regarding marketing activities and purchasing behaviors. It includes variables such as advertising expenses, email engagement, coupon usage, and total purchase amount. These data represent the marketing activity situation of a certain quarter. This dataset has been cleaned, with missing values removed, and all numerical variables have been correctly formatted. Duplicate records (if any) have been checked and deleted. Additionally, to enhance clarity and consistency, the variables have been renamed.

Figure 1 shows the distribution of marketing spend across customers. The distribution appears relatively even across the range, indicating that customers receive varying levels of marketing investment. There are no extreme outliers, suggesting that the data is suitable for further analysis of the relationship between marketing spend and purchase behaviour. Here, I have tried setting the bins to 20, 30, and 60. I found that 20 was too coarse, while 60 was too scattered. 30, however, seemed to be a more suitable value in my opinion.

According to Figure 2, we can observe that the blue trend line is upward, which indicates that the higher the marketing investment, the higher the purchase amount. However, the strength of the relationship is of moderate intensity, suggesting that although there is a relationship between the two, there are more than just this factor influencing the purchasing power of customer service. Nevertheless, this proves the effectiveness of the marketing strategy.

Data results

This discovery indicates that increasing marketing investment might be an effective strategy to boost consumer spending, but it should be combined with other methods to achieve the best results.
